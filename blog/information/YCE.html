YCE

YCE, 3 persons, one action. Shaped Ray's Idea, The Story of Neutra Lore, and the vision.

How and why, and why is it controversial?

Introduction

The ‘YCE’ is what shaped the ideas and the development of The Luminous Project, alternatively known as "Neutra Lore."

‘YCE’, however, isn't a simple concept or idea of what we think of. Before ‘YCE’, it started with ‘Y’; its roots began all the way from the earliest development days of Neutra Lore, before the V3R Development Team was formed. Then, another two changed, and the vision.

An idea of "three person, three uniqueness", after I realized, and became a source, contributed (directly or indirectly), driven to create new ideas, inspirations, and development. Yet, inspiration often comes with controversial topics, specifically a common topic called "shipping". What began as inspiration, admiration, and influence turned into misunderstandings, questions, debates about its meanings, and intentions.

In this blog post, we will be uncovering the history and intentions behind “Y,” “C,” and “E,” why they are important, what my opinion is on them, and how their influence continues throughout the developing journey.

How and Why

One of them has their own characters on Neutra Lore: "Sha Gajiko", "Jun Cess Anseonjin", and "Eliza Igancia". Not their names, but taken from the Neutra Lore Characters, which are connected to them. The reason why I chose or admire them is because of one action that caused a change in Ray's Idea and Development. Not something we all think of, but rather inspiration and compassion.

‘Y’, the FIRST person who shaped Ray's Idea of making Neutra Lore with one character. ‘C’, the SECOND person who admired the projects, which was named NSD:TC to NSD Anseonjin Training Center (ATC), and leading up to the creation of Project: Liwanag, which is formerly known as DNA-PC, an ongoing project of the core game. ‘E’, the THIRD person, in a swift replacement, I decided to make ‘E’ the 3rd because of potential problems that may arise about ‘C’. Also a friend, but on the other side. One message I stumbled upon back then, when no one said 'hi.' Then "E" replied, "hello." Somehow, out of nowhere, this led to the creation of the Quality of Life (QoL) Project, and so on.

During the development days of The Luminous Project, Neutra Lore (Take 1). The part where I need to come up with a new character, such as Ray, Ethan, and so on, that idea struck me when I was playing. A joke character that I made back then turned into a character for Neutra Lore. Though I felt that it’s not right to take its name, every time I make a character or come up with names, I do. So I decided to make changes to avoid directly mentioning or involving its name outright. Since the characters had no feelings or personality (unless given) at all, it is based on personality or behavior. It still doesn’t feel right, but it passed through checks, ensuring it is not directly.

As stated in the blog post rules, be mindful of personal information and secrets between each other! I respect their privacy, and you should respect yours too. Since they weren’t aware of The Luminous Project, I made it because of an idea, instead of being bored sometimes, and burnout from the tasks. A story that is really based on Ray JL’s life and then to the world's problems, but in a different dimension or universe.

A kind of something that writers wanted in their lives, but didn’t achieve when they were young.

A mix of genres in one project or a story. Maybe preferably, mostly all. Take 1 was really hard, actually, since you had to correct them all. Take 2 was fine, but a bit of a problem. Take 3 is what we’re going to solve with Take 1 and Take 2 of the Neutra Lore problem, and make it clear to an audience without knowing it. Learning from mistakes, determining the audience, the community, and so on.

Why is it controversial or issue?

We’re here with names that have issues, and that’s a risk I’ve thought about. The danger of silence, though, was greater. Rumors grew because I didn’t explain, and without clarity, people filled the gaps with their own assumptions.

My goal isn’t to spread those misunderstandings but to face them directly and then shift the focus to what “YCE” truly stands for—inspiration, not rumor. I don’t want the blog to revolve around negativity; most of it documents their positive impact on projects, with only a section dedicated to clearing up misconceptions for closure.

That’s why I avoid using their real names and instead transform their presence into characters, traits, or contributions. I’m not trying to “own” their actions or put them under a spotlight—it’s about recognizing the moments of inspiration that redirected my path. If any of them ever felt uncomfortable, I would respect that fully, because the purpose has never been to expose but to honor.

Consent matters, which is why I frame “YCE” less as individual identities and more as the roles they played in shaping creativity. What they sparked in me lives on in the projects, not as a spotlight on them personally.

There are also tangible milestones tied to their influence. “C” inspired the naming shift from NSD:TC to Anseonjin Training Center, also directly shaped Project: Liwanag’s direction. “Y” sparked the very first character concepts that evolved into Neutra Lore. “E”, through something as simple as replying “hello,” set off a chain that gave birth to the Quality of Life project. These aren’t imagined connections—they are traceable points in the timeline of my work.

At the same time, I acknowledge that inspiration is partly subjective. Legacy, however, isn’t built on objectivity or personal meaning alone; it’s the intersection of both. For me, “YCE” is that meeting point. The hardest part is knowing I can’t control how others interpret what I write, and I accept that misunderstandings may still arise. But this defense is the moment I draw a line between inspiration and rumor. My role is to set the record straight now so that the foundation isn’t misunderstood, and then let the projects stand on their own strength.

Neutra Lore, the games, the stories—these will carry the legacy forward without the need for constant explanations. Inspiration should outlast controversy, and that is what I want “YCE” to be remembered for.

If future readers find this blog post, and you know the meaning, the history, and so on. I want them to see that the record was set straight early on—that inspiration, not rumor, was the truth from the start. The safeguard is in the way I continue building. Neutra Lore, Project: Liwanag, and the rest of V3R’s work will be documented and shared on their own terms, with this defense being only one piece in a much larger body of history. Future readers won’t just see “YCE” tied to rumors—they’ll see “YCE” tied to creative milestones. The blog is structured so the focus remains on creativity, with the clarification as context, not centerpiece. In the long run, the projects themselves will overshadow the controversy, because they are the real evidence of what “YCE” stood for. This post is a bridge, not the destination—it closes one chapter so the work can speak louder than speculation. I see “YCE” not as a permanent label tied to individuals, but as a role they played in shaping creativity. It's important to make that balance explicit. The facts are there—naming changes, project directions, even the timing of small exchanges that sparked big outcomes. But what makes them meaningful is how I interpreted them.
